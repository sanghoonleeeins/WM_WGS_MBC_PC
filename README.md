**Visiaul Abstract** The direction of Waldenström Macroglobulinemia whole genome sequencing (WGS) data analysis\]

Using whole-genome sequencing (WGS) to 80x on 47 WM cases (24 newly diagnosed, 11 relapsed, and 12 unknown), we uncover WM subtype-specific single nucleotide variants (SNVs), single base substitution (SBS) signatures, somatic mutations, copy number abnormalities (CNAs), structural variants (SVs), and clonal phylogenies.

## Summary of this study

• Genomic Architecture of WM Subtypes: Whole-genome sequencing identifies MBC-like and PC-like Waldenström macroglobulinemia as genomically distinct subtypes with divergent mutational and evolutionary features.

• Subtype-Specific Genomic Landscapes: PC-like WM is characterized by significantly higher recurrence of 6q deletions (55%) and 1q copy number gains, whereas MBC-like cases have a higher mutational burden and a greater number of structural variants.

• Divergent Evolutionary Trajectories: Mapping recurrent deletions on 6q clarifies the evolutionary pathway of WM subtypes, providing a refined genomic framework for tailoring therapeutic strategies based on cellular differentiation states.

• Early Clonal Initiation of CNAs: Clonal phylogeny analysis reveals that 6q loss and 1q gain are predominantly early initiating events occurring at a similar molecular time point as MYD88 and CXCR4 mutations, challenging the previous suggestion that 6q loss is a late driver event.
